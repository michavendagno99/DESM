DATA SOURCES
============
Provenance notes for every dataset used in this study. The raw files
themselves are not distributed in this repository — see ../README.md for
why, and for where to obtain each one.


1. Worldwide Governance Indicators (WGI)
-----------------------------------------
File(s):    Data_Worldwide_Governance_Indicators_WB.xlsx
Publisher:  World Bank
Coverage:   200+ economies, 1996-2024, annual
Used for:   The institutional-quality index (q_i) used to estimate the
            governance coupling coefficient (gamma).

The WGI measures governance along six dimensions: Voice and
Accountability, Political Stability, Government Effectiveness, Regulatory
Quality, Rule of Law, and Control of Corruption. Each dimension is built
from perception data pooled across 35 cross-country sources (household
surveys, firm surveys, and expert assessments), which lets the WGI compare
governance quality across countries on a common scale even though no two
countries are evaluated by the same underlying survey.

The WGI reports each dimension two ways: a standardized score (mean 0,
unit variance across countries in a given year) and, since the 2025
edition, an absolute 0-100 score anchored to fixed benchmark countries so
that scores are comparable across years as well as across countries. The
2025 edition also revised data-source screening, indicator mapping, and
the aggregation model; all historical estimates back to 1996 were
recalculated under the new methodology for consistency.


2. World Development Indicators (WDI)
---------------------------------------
File(s):    Data_World_Development_Indicators_WB.xlsx
Publisher:  World Bank
Coverage:   200+ countries and territories, 1960-present, annual
Used for:   The core macroeconomic panel — GDP per capita, investment
            (gross fixed capital formation), and the other state and
            control variables the model is built on.

WDI is the World Bank's general-purpose development database: over 1,500
indicators spanning economic growth, education, health, poverty, trade,
energy, infrastructure, governance, and environmental sustainability,
compiled from national statistical agencies and international sources.

5. CEPII Gravity Database — services trade barriers (EAV)
--------------------------------------------------------------
File(s):    EAV_2004_2007_2011
Publisher:  CEPII
Coverage:   118 countries, 9 service sectors; reference years 2004, 2007,
            2011
Used for:   Ad-valorem-equivalent restrictions on cross-border services
            trade, as an instrument/control in the trade-openness channel.

Built on the GTAP services-trade database for 2011, extending the method
of Fontagné, Guillin, and Mitaritonna (2011) — who covered 64 countries in
2004 — to a larger country set, with 2004 and 2007 estimates included for
comparison.


6. Global Financial Development Database
--------------------------------------------
File(s):    20220909-global-financial-development-database
Publisher:  World Bank
Coverage:   214 economies, 1960-2021, annual (108 indicators)
Used for:   Private credit and other financial-depth variables.
Last updated: September 2022.

Organized around a "4x2" framework: depth, access, efficiency, and
stability, each measured separately for financial institutions (e.g.
banks, insurers) and financial markets (e.g. stock and bond markets). It
builds on and extends the World Bank's earlier "Financial Development and
Structure" database. See Cihak, Demirguc-Kunt, Feyen & Levine (2012),
"Benchmarking Financial Systems around the World," World Bank Policy
Research Working Paper 6175, for the methodology behind the framework.
The World Bank does not warrant the accuracy of this dataset; consult the
metadata tab and original data providers for point-level queries.


7. Quality of Government (QoG) Standard dataset
-----------------------------------------------------
File(s):    qog_std_cs_jan26
Publisher:  Quality of Government Institute, University of Gothenburg
Coverage:   ~2,100 variables from 100+ source datasets
Used for:   The composite governance cross-section, the settler-mortality
            instrument (ajr_settmort), and the colonial-origin instrument
            (ht_colonial) used in the instrumental-variables identification
            of gamma.

Two variants are relevant here: the cross-sectional (CS) version, which
takes each country's most recent available year (2022, falling back to
2023, then 2021, up to +/- 3 years if 2022 is missing), and the
time-series (TS) version, which covers 1946-2025 at the country-year
level. The QoG codebook documents every variable and its original source
in full. The dataset won the Lijphart/Przeworski/Verba Award for Best
Dataset (APSA Comparative Politics Section) in 2009.
