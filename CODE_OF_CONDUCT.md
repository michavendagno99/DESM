# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This repository supports an academic research project. We want
participation — issues, discussions, pull requests, and correspondence — to
be a positive, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of
career stage, institutional affiliation, nationality, or background.

## Expected behavior

- Engage in good faith, especially in scientific disagreement. Critique
  ideas, methods, and results; do not attack people.
- Back claims with evidence — a citation, a reproduction of a result, a
  concrete counterexample — rather than assertion.
- Be respectful of the time and effort behind the work, including in
  disagreement with its conclusions.
- Accept that the manuscript's content is the author's responsibility and
  is presently moving through peer review; substantive scientific concerns
  are best raised through direct correspondence (see `README.md`) or formal
  peer review channels, not through repository contributions that alter the
  manuscript.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments directed at
  contributors.
- Harassment in any form, public or private.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of issues, discussions, or reviews.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within all repository spaces (issues, pull
requests, discussions) and in direct correspondence conducted on behalf of
the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to the corresponding
author at mstephen-arias@javeriana.edu.co. Reports will be reviewed and
addressed as appropriate to the situation, which may include a warning,
temporary restriction, or removal from repository spaces.
